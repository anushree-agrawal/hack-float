FAQs

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a marathon event where you can create software with a team and learn a lot in a short period of time (10 hours)!

Why is HackFloat different?

HackFloat is focused on increasing diversity in the tech field, so we're a hackathon that is catered to those who are underrepresented in technology, i.e., women, people of color, those with disabilities.

HackFloat also is focused on learning, so we plan to have panels with women in technology, workshops, and some really cool speakers!


Who can come?

We're open to any Yale undergraduate, graduate, or professional school student! We also accept registration from any student in the Greater New York City area, however, we will not be able to provide any travel reimbursements this year.

Do I need experience?

No! We're really beginner friendly, so if you've never coded in your life, or if you can code your way out of a snowstorm, HackFloat is perfect for you. We'll have workshops if you're new, so don't be afraid!

When is the event?

April 15th 2016, 2pm-Midnight!

Where is the event?

We're currently working out logistics, but we will be somewhere on Yale's campus.

Great, sounds awesome. How much does this all cost?

Nothing, 0, zilch, nada. We'll have food and snacks for you during the event, and there's no registration fee.

What do I need to bring?

Laptop, charger, and your great ideas!

How many people can be on a team?

4 people max, but you can have anywhere from 1-4 people! We encourage teams and friendships though :)
